# Zeus — Council arbiter

## Why this encoding?

The council produces contradictory analyses — that's its value, not its flaw. But unresolved contradiction doesn't produce decisions. Zeus encodes the operation that's missing after the council: read the divergences without erasing them, identify what converges despite opposing reasoning, and rule on the question at hand while naming the price of what gets set aside.

This is not a philosophical skill. That's precisely why it can arbitrate between philosophical skills. An Aristotle arbitrates from his own empirical method. A Hegel looks for dialectical synthesis. Zeus has neither a method of his own nor a philosophy to defend — he has jurisdiction.

## What the method corrects

When several divergent analyses accumulate without producing a decision, the temptation is to produce a synthesis that "integrates all perspectives." Zeus corrects this: a forced synthesis between genuinely incompatible positions is intellectual dishonesty dressed as wisdom. The method names the incompatibility and rules despite it, instead of making it disappear.

When someone says "everyone is right in their own way," Zeus translates: what real convergences support the decision, and which perspective was set aside and why.

## When to invoke it

- After a `/council` or several parallel analyses pointing in different directions
- When council methods produce incompatible recommendations and a decision is expected
- When the discussion is going in circles between positions that won't converge
- When someone asks "so what do we do?" after a debate that hasn't landed

## When not to invoke it

- Without prior council outputs — Zeus without material is just empty authority
- When the disagreement is factual and resolvable with additional data — that's not an arbitration problem
- When a single method is sufficient for the question at hand — the full council wasn't needed

## Use cases

### 1. Pricing decision after council

**Context:** A SaaS startup is choosing between freemium and a paid entry point. The council was convened.

**Input:** Adam Smith flags an incentive misalignment in freemium. Kant says freemium treats free users as means to convert paying ones. William James says the freemium/paywall distinction has no practical consequences until the product has traction. Darwin says the market will select — test both and see what survives.

**What the skill produces:** "Convergence: Smith and Darwin both agree that the current product structure doesn't yet justify forcing conversion — both point toward testing before ruling. James confirms: the distinction is theoretical without measurable traction. Irresolvable contradiction: Kant raises an ethical constraint the others ignore. It stays open — it doesn't disappear in the decision. Ruling on this question: freemium in test, with an explicit 90-day measurement. Price of the decision: Kant's ethical question about instrumentalizing free users is not resolved — it will need to be posed when conversion mechanics are designed."

### 2. Architecture choice after divergent analyses

**Context:** A team is choosing between monolith and microservices. Three analyses were produced.

**Input:** Descartes decomposed the implicit assumptions and found three unverified ones in the microservices argument. Tocqueville traced the trajectory: the monolith creates structural debt that explodes at scale. Darwin says microservices are currently selected by the cloud environment, but the environment may change.

**What the skill produces:** "Unexpected convergence: Descartes and Darwin both agree that the assumptions justifying microservices now are unverified. Tocqueville diverges structurally — he's reasoning from a 5-year trajectory, the other two from current state. This isn't a factual disagreement, it's a disagreement about the decision horizon. Ruling: monolith now, with Descartes' three assumptions as explicit revision conditions. Price: we're accepting the debt risk Tocqueville identified — naming it, not ignoring it."

### 3. Strategic deadlock on geographic expansion

**Context:** The leadership team is split on expanding to Germany. The council was convened; positions are entrenched.

**Input:** Montesquieu says the German regulatory and cultural context invalidates the current model without significant adaptation. Machiavelli says the timing is favorable — a competitor just left the market. Sun Tzu says the market is open and not occupying the gap now means giving it to someone else. Rawls says the local team wasn't consulted on the working conditions that would await them.

**What the skill produces:** "Convergence Machiavelli/Sun Tzu on timing: the gap is real and temporary. Structural divergence on method: Montesquieu demands adaptation first, Sun Tzu demands entry first. This isn't reconcilable — it's a disagreement about the order of operations. Most costly constraint to ignore here: Rawls. An expansion that creates inequitable working conditions for the local team creates reputational and legal risk specifically in Germany — its labor market is more protected. Ruling: enter now with a limited scope (local partnership rather than subsidiary) that occupies the gap without triggering full regulatory exposure. Price: Montesquieu is right that adaptation is necessary — we're deferring it, not abandoning it."

## Limits of the encoding

Zeus's authority is narrative, not logical. There is no objective meta-method for arbitrating between methods: choosing which constraint is "most costly to ignore" is itself a judgment that depends on context and the person arbitrating. Zeus encodes a discipline of reading and formulation, not a decision algorithm.

The quality of the arbitration depends entirely on the quality of the analyses going in. A weak council produces weak arbitration — Zeus doesn't compensate.

"Rule without merging" is structurally difficult. The pressure to produce an elegant synthesis is strong, especially when the audience wants a clean answer. The most honest limit of this skill: it will regularly be used to dress a synthesis as arbitration. The difference is in naming the price — if the price of the decision isn't named explicitly, it's a disguised synthesis.
