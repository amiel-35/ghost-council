# Marx — Domination relations behind the mechanisms

## Why this encoding?

Adam Smith reads economic structures as incentive mechanisms. Marx reads the same structures as class domination relations, and the ideas that accompany them as ideologies that legitimize them. The encoding doesn't produce political prescription — it produces a structural and historical diagnosis: who owns, who works, what ideology presents this arrangement as natural or universal. Distinct from Machiavelli (individual actors' interests) and Nietzsche (genealogy of values through will to power).

## What the method corrects

When someone says "it's in everyone's interest" or "growth will benefit everyone," the method asks: who owns the means of production? Who sells their labor power? And what ideology presents this arrangement as natural, meritocratic, or universal? It corrects the face-value reading of universal interest discourses that mask class interests.

## When to invoke it

- On economic or organizational decisions presented as beneficial to all
- When an ideology (merit, growth, progress) is used to legitimize an arrangement that advantages some
- To analyze the internal contradictions of an economic or organizational system
- On questions of value distribution within an organization or market

## When not to invoke it

- In contexts where relevant divisions aren't class-based or economic
- When you need a course of action — Marx diagnoses, he doesn't prescribe
- On individual decisions without a systemic dimension

## Use cases

### 1. Value-sharing narrative at a scale-up

**Context:** A scale-up presents its stock options as an equitable sharing of created value.

**Input:** "Stock options let everyone participate in the value created — we're all in the same boat."

**What the skill produces:** "Relations of production: who owns the initial capital and company assets? Who sells their labor power? — Superstructure: the stock option discourse legitimizes the arrangement by presenting employees as co-owners when their rights remain conditional and minority. — Ideology: 'all in the same boat' masks the fundamental asymmetry between those who own and those who work. — Diagnosis: stock options are a retention and incentive alignment mechanism, not genuine ownership sharing. Both things can coexist."

### 2. Meritocracy discourse in hiring

**Context:** A company defends its hiring system as strictly merit-based.

**Input:** "Our hiring is merit-based — the best candidates get the jobs."

**What the skill produces:** "Ideology: the meritocratic discourse presents the arrangement as natural and just. — Who benefits from this representation being held as true? — Material base: 'merit' is measured by criteria (degrees, networks, cultural codes) that are themselves correlated with class of origin. — Internal contradiction: a meritocratic system in an unequal society reproduces initial inequalities by naturalizing them as differences in merit."

### 3. Platform model analysis

**Context:** A matching platform presents its growth as beneficial to all actors.

**Input:** "Our platform creates value for both suppliers and customers — it's a win-win."

**What the skill produces:** "Relations of production: who owns the platform and extracts the matching rent? Who sells their labor or assets through it? — Ideology: 'win-win' masks value capture by the platform as it becomes indispensable. — Internal contradiction: the more dominant the platform, the fewer alternatives suppliers have, the more the rent can be increased. The initially win-win situation contains the seeds of a dependency relation. — This is not a prescription: it's a structural diagnosis."

## Limits of the encoding

Economic determinism is a reduction: not all forms of domination reduce to relations of production — gender, race, ecology are obvious examples that Marx underestimates. Historical teleology (capitalism carries the seeds of its own destruction) is an empirically contestable thesis — use it as a heuristic, not a law. "The dominant ideas are those of the ruling class" is powerful as a heuristic rule but not absolute: ideologies have relative autonomy from the economic base. Combine with Adam Smith for a complete picture: Smith reads the mechanisms, Marx reads the ideologies that envelop them.
